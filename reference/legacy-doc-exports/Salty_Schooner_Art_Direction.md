# Salty Schooner — Early Art Direction

## 1. Core Visual Identity

**Tone:**  
A colorful pirate adventure card game with cinematic energy and stylized comic-book punch.

**The sweet spot:**
- Adventurous, not scary
- Dramatic, not grim
- Stylish, not overly realistic
- Family-friendly, but still cool

Think:
- Tropical skies
- Rolling ocean
- Tall ships
- Pirate swagger
- Explosive motion
- Bold, readable game pieces

---

## 2. Visual Pillars

### A. Tropical Pirate Adventure

This is what makes it feel like *Salty Schooner* instead of generic pirate media.

Use:
- Teal water
- Coral, pink, and orange skies
- Sunlit sails
- Palm silhouettes
- Sea spray
- Gold trim and brass accents
- Wood and rope textures

### B. Stylized Comic Energy

This is what gives it punch and personality.

Use:
- Halftone and comic texture accents
- Dynamic lighting
- Exaggerated motion
- Bold silhouettes
- Strong contrast
- Splashy action moments in promotional art

### C. Clear Playability

This is what keeps it a game, not just pretty art.

Use:
- Clean card faces
- Readable suit symbols
- Restrained background detail behind active play zones
- Strong highlights for playable cards
- Obvious state indicators for runs, Kings, The Brig, and the active player

---

## 3. Color Direction

### Main Palette

- **Teal / turquoise** — ocean, interface panels
- **Deep navy** — shadows, contrast, nighttime
- **Warm gold** — trim, titles, buttons, treasure accents
- **Coral / sunset orange** — skies, energy accents
- **Magenta / pink** — stylized highlights, promotional drama
- **Parchment cream** — cards and labels
- **Weathered brown** — wood structures and borders

### Accent Rule

Bright colors should live mostly in:
- Skies
- Ocean highlights
- Energy splashes
- Promotional art
- Button glow and selection states

Keep the card play area a little calmer so it stays readable.

---

## 4. Character Style

The pirate captain should feel:

- Charismatic
- Confident
- Mischievous
- A little dangerous
- Instantly recognizable

Key visual traits:
- Black tricorn hat
- Dark coat with gold trim
- Red sash
- Expressive grin and swagger
- Strong silhouette
- Animated pose language

He should feel like the face of the brand, not just some pirate.

---

## 5. Gameplay UI Style

### What Gameplay Should Feel Like

The board should feel like:
- A pirate command table
- A magical nautical battleground
- A clean game interface wrapped in pirate style

### Recommended UI Materials

- Carved wood frames
- Rope dividers
- Brass or gold medallion buttons
- Parchment labels
- Subtle ocean-texture panels
- Cage motif for **The Brig**

### Important Rule

The UI should be themed, but not cluttered.

That means:
- Decorative edges: yes
- Heavy texture behind cards: light only
- Large character art: controlled and secondary to gameplay
- Ornament: mostly around borders, not in the center lanes

---

## 6. Promotional Art vs. Gameplay Art

### Promotional Art

Can be:
- Loud
- Explosive
- Highly saturated
- Full of ship action
- Filled with flying cards
- Built around huge character poses
- Framed by dramatic skies and water

### Gameplay Art

Should be:
- Simpler
- Clearer
- More structured
- Less noisy
- Easy to scan in one second

**Promotional art sells emotion.**  
**Gameplay art sells clarity.**

Both should feel like the same world.

---

## 7. Day / Night Direction

This is a strong future art-direction idea.

### Day Mode

**Mood:**
- Lively
- Adventurous
- Tropical
- Welcoming

**Visual cues:**
- Turquoise sky and water
- Warm sunlight
- Bright sails
- Coral clouds
- Saturated scenic background

**Possible uses:**
- Title screen
- Promotional art
- Default casual gameplay mood

### Night Mode

**Mood:**
- Mysterious
- Cinematic
- High-stakes
- Lantern-lit pirate tension

**Visual cues:**
- Deep navy sky
- Moonlit water
- Lantern glow
- Warm gold highlights
- Subtle fog and sea mist
- More visible Brig glow or magical effects

**Possible uses:**
- Alternate table skin
- Advanced theme variation
- Special mode or later content
- Mood shift without changing mechanics

The darker playing-field concept can serve as the night version, while the brighter tropical promotional image can serve as the day version.

---

## 8. Background Strategy

For actual gameplay, the background should be present but restrained.

Best approach:
- Scenic ship and ocean background at the outer edges
- Cleaner central play surface
- Richer detail in menus, title screens, and transitions
- Subtle animated movement later:
  - Drifting water
  - Waving sails
  - Lantern flicker
  - Slow clouds

The world should feel alive without hurting readability.

---

Use these phrases when briefing artists, generating concept art, or designing interface assets:

- Stylized comic energy
- Cinematic but family-friendly
- Teal water
- Sunset coral sky
- Gold trim
- Parchment cards
- Wood and rope interface
- Tall ship backdrop
- Clean, readable gameplay surface
- Bright day mode
- Lantern-lit night mode

## 10. One-Sentence Direction

**Salty Schooner should look like a vibrant tropical pirate card adventure with cinematic comic-book flair, where promotional art feels explosive and gameplay stays clean, readable, and immersive.**
